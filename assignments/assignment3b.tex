\section{EWA as generalised Bayes:}
\label{sec:org68149b0}

Consider the following special case for prediction with expert advice:

At time \(t\):
\begin{itemize}
\item Each ex\mrk{}pert \(i \in A\) makes probabilistic pred\mrk{}ictions \(p_{i,t}(x)\) for every possible \(x \in X\).
\item You make a choice of some expert \(a_t \in A\)
\item You observe \(x_t \in X\)
\item You obtain reward \(r_t = - \ln p_{a_t, t}(x)\)
\item You calculate the rew\mrk{}ards of all experts, \(r_{i,t} = - \ln p_{i, t}(x)\).
\end{itemize}

Show that the EWA algo\mrk{}rithm, which updates the weights according to:
\[
w_{t+1}(i)
=
\frac{w_t(j) \exp[\eta r_{i,t})]}{\sum_{j} w_t(j) \exp[\eta r_{j,t})]}
\]
Corresponds to a modified poster\mrk{}ior update of the form,
\[
\bel_{t+1}(i) = \frac{P_i(x_t)^eta \bel_t(i)}{\sum_j P_j(x_t)^eta \bel_t(j)}
\]
where \(\eta > 0\) changes the importance of the likelihood function.
